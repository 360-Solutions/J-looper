Welcome to J-looper

Building a looper app that works on a typical smartphone without an audio interface or wired headphones comes with a unique challenge: preventing the microphone from hearing and recording the app's own backing track (audio bleed).

The J-looper Compromise:
To solve this, the backing track drops out the moment you hit record. It’s like a spotlight performance where the band stops and you play a solo phrase. To stay on beat, you'll need to rely on your internal rhythm and the visual metronome—the white node traveling across the grid.

It’s a trade-off that not only makes wireless looping possible, but actively develops your internal timing skills.

Note: Like a classic analog stompbox, J-looper lives in the moment. Your audio loops are not saved permanently when you refresh the page. Have fun and don't take it too seriously!
